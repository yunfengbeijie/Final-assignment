Red panda

Vulnerable species

They usually live in large tree holes or stone holes and rock crevices. In the morning and evening, they come out for activity and forage. During the day, they sleep in caves or deep in the shade of trees. Sleep when the head curled up in the limbs, forelimbs cling to the head, with the tail covered in the body.

Leopardus pardalis

No endangered species

They live in dense tropical rain forests, in the high mountains of the Andes at 3,800 meters, in dense shrubs, in thinly vegetated semi-desert areas, in coastal mangroves and so on, but are rarely found in the open.

Loxodonta

Endangered species

African elephants have very large ears that can be up to 1.5 meters long. It has five feet on the front and three feet on the back, 21 pairs of ribs and up to 26 tailbones.
