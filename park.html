Banff National Park

Established in 1885, Banff National Park is Canada's oldest national park. It is located in the northern section of the Rocky Mountains, approximately 110-180 km west of Calgary, Alberta, Canada. Covering 6,641 square kilometers, the park is covered with glaciers, ice fields, pine forests and mountains.

The Canadian Trans-Highway passes through Banff National Park, starting at its eastern border at Canmore, crossing Banff and Lake Louise to reach Yew Crane National Park in British Columbia.

The Lake Louise Castle Hotel is located 54 kilometers west of the town of Banff in the Lake Louise Valley. Located 15 kilometers from Lake Louise and surrounded by the Ten Peaks, the lake is reflected in the beautiful scenery and was adopted on the back of the Canadian $20 bill from 1969 to 1979. The Lake Louise Mountain Resort is also located near the valley.
